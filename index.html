About Me

Hey - my name is Steven Leone, and I am a master's student in Statistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. My research interests include deep learning, natural language processing, and large language model length generalization.

Before Michigan, I studied automated algorithms at Georgia Tech, where I led a team of undergraduate researchers using neural architecture search to improve neural network designs on question answering tasks. After graduating, I worked as a software engineer at Amazon and Mercor for approximately three years.

At the University of Michigan, I have expanded my computer science background with graduate-level statistics, studying Bayesian modeling, model flexibility and complexity, time series analysis, and statistical learning. I have been combining rigorous statistical methodology with modern machine learning and deep learning techniques to solve challenging real-world problems.

My recent research includes convolutional neural network based models for detecting galaxy clusters in the DES DR2 survey with Dr. Jeffrey Regier, researching length generalization in large language models such as T5, BART, and LLaMA, and comparing Bayesian and classical machine learning approaches for wildfire detection.

I also study foreign languages (German, Spanish, etc.). I achieved > 99% accuracy on the Arabic POS Tagging dataset and am currently exploring deep learning approaches for Arabic propaganda classification.
